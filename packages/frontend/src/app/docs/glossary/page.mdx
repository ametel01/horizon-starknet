# Glossary

Key terms used in Horizon Protocol.

---

## A

### Advanced Mode
Interface mode for experienced users, providing PT/YT trading, manual minting, and full market controls. See [Simple vs Advanced Mode](/docs/getting-started#simple-vs-advanced-mode).

### AMM (Automated Market Maker)
A smart contract that enables trading without order books. Horizon's AMM is optimized for time-decaying assets like PT. See [AMM Mechanics](/docs/how-it-works/amm-mechanics).

### APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
Simple interest rate without compounding. <Formula>{"\\text{APR} = r \\times t"}</Formula>

### APY (Annual Percentage Yield)
Interest rate with compounding. <Formula>{"\\text{APY} = (1 + r)^t - 1"}</Formula>. Horizon displays APY for all yields. See [APY Calculation](/docs/mechanics/apy-calculation).

---

## C

### Claim All
A batch operation that collects all pending rewards and accrued yield across multiple positions in a single transaction. Available in the Portfolio view to save gas compared to claiming individually.

---

## D

### Depeg
When an asset loses its expected value relationship. For yield tokens, this could mean the underlying asset no longer maintains its peg to the base asset.

### Discount
The difference between PT price and 1.0. A PT at 0.95 has a 5% discount, which becomes your return at maturity.

---

## E

### Expiry
See [Maturity](#maturity).

### Expiry Divisor
A factory parameter that enforces expiry date alignment. When set (e.g., 86400 for daily alignment), all market expiries must be evenly divisible by this value. Used to standardize expiry dates across markets. A value of 0 disables alignment checks.

---

## F

### Faucet
A testnet utility that distributes free test tokens for development and testing purposes. On devnet, the Faucet provides mock yield-bearing tokens to experiment with the protocol without real funds.

### Fee Decay
The automatic reduction of swap fees as a market approaches maturity. Fees scale linearly with time-to-expiry: a 6-month market pays roughly half the fee rate of a 1-year market, and fees reach zero at expiry. See [AMM Mechanics](/docs/how-it-works/amm-mechanics#fee-decay).

### Fixed Yield
A guaranteed return locked in at the time of purchase. Achieved by buying PT at a discount and holding to maturity. See [Earn Fixed Yield](/docs/guides/earn-fixed-yield).

### Flash Mint
An atomic operation that mints PT and YT from SY, uses them within the same transaction, and returns the required tokens. Enables capital-efficient arbitrage and complex DeFi strategies without upfront capital. See [Flash Mint Mechanics](/docs/mechanics/flash-mint).

---

## H

### hrzSTRK
Horizon's wrapped staked STRK token. An SY (Standardized Yield) wrapper around sSTRK that enables yield tokenization within the Horizon protocol. Users deposit sSTRK to receive hrzSTRK, which can then be split into PT and YT.

---

## I

### IFlashCallback
A contract interface that must be implemented to use Flash Mint. The YT contract calls your callback function with the minted tokens, and you must return the required SY by the end of the transaction. See [Flash Mint Mechanics](/docs/mechanics/flash-mint).

### IL (Impermanent Loss)
The difference between LP returns and simply holding the underlying assets. Occurs when asset prices diverge from entry point. See [Risks](/docs/risks#lp-risks).

### Implied Yield
The annualized return implied by the current PT price. See [Pricing](/docs/mechanics/pricing).

<Formula display>{"\\text{Implied APY} = \\left( \\frac{1}{P_{PT}} \\right)^{\\frac{365}{d}} - 1"}</Formula>

### Interest Fee Rate
The percentage of accrued yield that the protocol collects as a fee. Applied when YT holders claim their accumulated interest. Typical value: 3%. For example, if YT earns 100 tokens of interest and the fee rate is 3%, the holder receives 97 tokens.

---

## L

### Liquidity
The ease of trading an asset. Higher liquidity means lower price impact and tighter spreads.

### Liquidity Provider (LP)
Someone who deposits assets into an AMM pool to earn trading fees. See [Provide Liquidity](/docs/guides/provide-liquidity).

### LP Token
A token representing your share of a liquidity pool. Used to withdraw your portion of the pool plus earned fees.

---

## M

### Maturity
The date when PT can be redeemed for the underlying asset and YT expires. All Horizon markets have a fixed maturity date.

### Minting
Creating PT and YT by depositing SY. You receive equal amounts of PT and YT.

---

## P

### PnL (Profit and Loss)
The net gain or loss on a position, calculated as the difference between current value and cost basis. Displayed in Portfolio view for each position, showing both absolute value and percentage return.

### Portfolio Value
The total USD value of all positions held in Horizon, including SY, PT, YT, and LP tokens. Updated in real-time based on current market prices and accrued yield.

### Price Impact
How much your trade moves the market price. Large trades in low-liquidity pools have high price impact.

### Principal Token (PT)
A token redeemable for 1 unit of underlying at maturity. Trades at a discount before maturity. The discount represents your fixed yield. See [Yield Tokens](/docs/how-it-works/yield-tokens).

### PY Index
The exchange rate used for minting and redemption. Always uses the maximum historical rate to protect PT holders.

<Formula display>{"\\text{Index}_{PY} = \\max(\\text{rate}_{current}, \\text{Index}_{stored})"}</Formula>

---

## R

### Redemption
Converting PT back to underlying assets. After maturity: PT → underlying (1:1). Before maturity: PT + YT → SY. See [Redemption](/docs/mechanics/redemption).

### Reward Fee Rate
The percentage of reward tokens that the protocol collects as a fee. Applied when users claim reward tokens distributed by SYWithRewards implementations. Typical value: 3%. Separate from the interest fee rate, which applies to base yield.

### Reward Tokens
Additional tokens distributed to SY holders beyond the base yield. Some yield-bearing assets provide incentive rewards (e.g., protocol tokens, governance tokens) that are separate from the underlying yield. Only SY tokens that implement the SYWithRewards interface support reward token distribution.

---

## S

### Simple Mode
Default interface mode for new users, featuring streamlined deposit/withdraw and automatic yield optimization. See [Simple vs Advanced Mode](/docs/getting-started#simple-vs-advanced-mode).

### Slippage
The difference between expected and actual execution price. Set slippage tolerance to limit maximum acceptable deviation.

### Spot Price
The current market price for immediate execution (before accounting for price impact).

### Standardized Yield (SY)
A wrapper token that standardizes different yield-bearing assets for use in Horizon. SY can be split into PT + YT.

### SYWithRewards
An extended SY implementation that supports distributing reward tokens in addition to the base yield. SY tokens for assets like sSTRK use this interface to pass through staking rewards and other incentives to holders. See [Reward Tokens](#reward-tokens).

### Starknet
An Ethereum Layer 2 network using ZK rollups. Horizon is deployed on Starknet.

### STRK
The native token of Starknet, used for gas fees.

---

## T

### Time Decay
The gradual decrease in YT value as maturity approaches. As less time remains, less yield can be collected.

### Token Aggregator
A service that sources liquidity across multiple DEXs to find optimal swap routes. Horizon integrates with aggregators like AVNU for efficient token swaps when converting between assets.

### TVL (Total Value Locked)
The total value of assets deposited in a protocol or pool.

---

## U

### Underlying Asset
The base yield-bearing token (e.g., sSTRK). SY wraps the underlying, and PT redeems for it at maturity.

### Underlying Yield
The current yield being generated by the underlying asset. May differ from implied yield.

### Unwrap
The process of converting SY tokens back to the underlying yield-bearing asset. For example, unwrapping hrzSTRK returns sSTRK. The inverse of wrapping/depositing into SY.

---

## V

### Variable Yield
Returns that fluctuate based on market conditions. YT holders receive variable yield from the underlying asset.

---

## Y

### Yield Bearing Token
A token that generates yield over time (e.g., sSTRK generates staking rewards).

### Yield Token (YT)
A token representing the right to collect yield from 1 unit of underlying until maturity. Expires worthless at maturity. See [Yield Tokens](/docs/how-it-works/yield-tokens).
