# FAQ

Common questions about Horizon Protocol.

---

## General

### What is Horizon?

Horizon is a yield tokenization protocol on Starknet. It lets you split yield-bearing assets into separate principal and yield components, enabling fixed-yield strategies and yield trading.

### How is this different from just holding a yield-bearing token?

With regular yield tokens, your returns vary with market rates. Horizon lets you:
- **Lock in fixed yields** by buying PT at a discount
- **Speculate on yields** by buying YT for leveraged exposure
- **Earn fees** by providing liquidity

### Is Horizon audited?

No. Horizon contracts have not been audited. Use with caution and only deposit what you can afford to lose.

---

## Tokens

### What is PT?

Principal Token. It represents your right to redeem 1 unit of the underlying asset at maturity. PT trades at a discount before maturity — the discount is your fixed yield. See [Yield Tokens](/docs/how-it-works/yield-tokens) for details.

### What is YT?

Yield Token. It represents your right to collect all yield generated by 1 unit of underlying until maturity. YT expires worthless at maturity. See [Yield Tokens](/docs/how-it-works/yield-tokens) for details.

### What is SY?

Standardized Yield. A wrapper that makes different yield-bearing tokens compatible with Horizon. SY can be split into PT + YT, and PT + YT can be combined back into SY. See [Glossary](/docs/glossary#s) for more terms.

### Why does PT trade at a discount?

The discount compensates you for giving up yield rights (which go to YT holders). At maturity, PT is worth exactly 1.0, so buying at 0.95 gives you a 5.26% return.

### Why does YT lose value over time?

YT's value comes from future yield. As time passes, less yield remains to collect, so YT value decreases toward zero at maturity.

## Trading

### How do I lock in a fixed yield?

Buy PT and hold until maturity. Your fixed yield equals:

<Formula display>{"\\text{Fixed APY} = \\left( \\frac{1}{P_{PT}} \\right)^{\\frac{365}{d}} - 1"}</Formula>

See [Earn Fixed Yield](/docs/guides/earn-fixed-yield) for the full guide.

### Can I exit early?

Yes, but at market prices:
- **Sell PT** on the AMM (may be profit or loss depending on yield changes)
- **Combine PT + YT** to get SY back (need equal amounts)

### What affects implied yield?

- Trading activity (buy PT = lower yield, sell PT = higher yield)
- Underlying yield changes
- Market sentiment
- Time to maturity

### What is slippage?

The difference between expected and actual execution price. Set slippage tolerance in settings to protect against unfavorable price movements.

## Liquidity

### What do LPs earn?

- **Swap fees** from trades
- **Underlying yield** on the SY portion
- **PT appreciation** as maturity approaches

See [Provide Liquidity](/docs/guides/provide-liquidity) for the full LP guide.

### What is impermanent loss?

When implied yield moves significantly, LPs may have less value than if they had just held PT + SY separately. This is called impermanent loss (IL). See [Risks](/docs/risks#lp-risks) for details.

### When is IL lowest?

- Near maturity (PT converges to 1)
- When implied yield is stable
- In high-volume pools (fees offset IL)

## Maturity

### What happens at maturity?

- **PT** can be redeemed 1:1 for the underlying asset
- **YT** expires worthless (claim yield before expiry)
- **LP positions** should be withdrawn

### Can I redeem PT before maturity?

Only if you also have matching YT. Together, PT + YT can be redeemed for SY at any time. Otherwise, sell PT on the market.

### What if I forget to claim YT yield?

Unclaimed yield remains claimable until maturity. After maturity, YT expires and any unclaimed yield is lost.

<Callout type="warning">
Set calendar reminders for maturity dates. Claim YT yield and redeem PT before expiry.
</Callout>

## Technical

### What chain is Horizon on?

Starknet — an Ethereum L2 using ZK rollups. You need STRK for gas fees.

### Which wallets work?

Any Starknet-compatible wallet:
- Ready (ex Argent)
- Braavos

### What yield tokens are supported?

Currently Horizon is in experimental mode with mock tokens only. No real-value assets are supported yet.

Real yield-bearing tokens (nstSTRK, sSTRK, wstETH) will be added in future releases.

### How do I get test tokens?

Use the [Test Token Faucet](/faucet) to get free hrzSTRK tokens. You can mint 100 tokens once every 24 hours. These are mock tokens with no real value, designed for testing the protocol safely.

### How are prices determined?

The AMM uses a time-weighted curve that expresses prices as implied yield. As maturity approaches, the curve naturally pushes PT toward 1.0. See [AMM Mechanics](/docs/how-it-works/amm-mechanics) and [Pricing](/docs/mechanics/pricing) for technical details.

## Troubleshooting

### Transaction failed — what do I check?

1. Sufficient STRK for gas
2. Token approvals granted
3. Slippage not exceeded
4. Wallet connected to correct network

### Why is my yield lower than expected?

- Underlying yield may have decreased
- You may have entered at a different implied yield than expected
- Check the actual vs implied yield comparison

### Position not showing in portfolio?

- Refresh the page
- Check wallet is connected
- Verify transaction completed on explorer
