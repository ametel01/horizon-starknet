# Yield Tokens

When you deposit, Horizon creates two tokens from your position. Here's what each one does.

---

## Principal Token (PT)

**Your guaranteed principal.** PT redeems for exactly 1 unit of underlying at maturity.

  <TableHeader>
    <TableRow>
      <TableHead>Property</TableHead>
      <TableHead>Value</TableHead>

  <TableBody>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>At maturity</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Redeems 1:1 for underlying</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Before maturity</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Trades at a discount</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Your yield</TableCell>
      <TableCell>The discount IS your fixed return</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </TableBody>
</Table>

**Example:** Buy PT at 0.95. At maturity, redeem for 1.00. That's 5.26% profit, locked in.

<Callout type="tip">
PT is ideal if you want **predictable returns** and can hold until maturity.
</Callout>

---

## Yield Token (YT)

**Your yield rights.** YT collects all variable yield from the underlying until maturity.

<Table>
  <TableHeader>
    <TableRow>
      <TableHead>Property</TableHead>
      <TableHead>Value</TableHead>
    </TableRow>
  </TableHeader>
  <TableBody>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Before maturity</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Collects ongoing yield</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>At maturity</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Expires worthless</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
    <TableRow>
      <TableCell>Leverage</TableCell>
      <TableCell>Small cost, large yield exposure</TableCell>
    </TableRow>
  </TableBody>
</Table>

**Example:** If YT costs 5% of underlying, you get 20x exposure to yield changes.

<Callout type="warning">
YT is higher risk. If yield drops, YT can lose most of its value before maturity.
</Callout>

---

## Standardized Yield (SY)

SY is the wrapper that makes different yield tokens compatible with Horizon. You don't interact with it directly in Simple mode.

---

## The Key Equation

```
PT + YT = SY (always, before maturity)
```

This is enforced by arbitrage. If prices drift, traders profit by splitting or combining until balance is restored.
