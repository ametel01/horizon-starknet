# AMM Mechanics

Horizon uses a **logit-based AMM curve** specifically designed for trading time-decaying assets like PT. This curve ensures PT naturally converges to its redemption value at expiry.

---

## How Trading Works

The AMM pairs **PT** with **SY** (not YT directly). YT price is derived:

<Formula display>{"P_{YT} = 1 - P_{PT}"}</Formula>

When you trade:
- **Buy PT** → PT price rises, implied yield drops
- **Sell PT** → PT price falls, implied yield rises

---

## The Core Curve: Logit-Based Pricing

Unlike traditional constant-product AMMs (x × y = k), Horizon uses a **logit-based exchange rate**:

<Formula display>{"\\text{exchangeRate} = \\frac{\\ln\\left(\\frac{p}{1-p}\\right)}{\\text{rateScalar}} + \\text{rateAnchor}"}</Formula>

Where:
- <Formula>{"p"}</Formula> = proportion of PT in the pool: <Formula>{"\\frac{\\text{PT}}{\\text{PT} + \\text{SY}}"}</Formula>
- <Formula>{"\\text{rateScalar}"}</Formula> = time-adjusted sensitivity parameter
- <Formula>{"\\text{rateAnchor}"}</Formula> = dynamic anchor maintaining rate continuity

The **logit function** <Formula>{"\\ln(p/(1-p))"}</Formula> maps proportions to an unbounded range, creating smooth price curves that handle extreme market conditions gracefully.

---

## Rate Scalar: Time-Adjusted Sensitivity

The rate scalar controls how sensitive prices are to pool balance changes:

<Formula display>{"\\text{rateScalar} = \\text{scalarRoot} \\times \\frac{\\text{SECONDS\\_PER\\_YEAR}}{\\text{timeToExpiry}}"}</Formula>

As expiry approaches:
- <Formula>{"\\text{timeToExpiry} \\to 0"}</Formula>
- <Formula>{"\\text{rateScalar} \\to \\infty"}</Formula>
- **Curve flattens** — trades have minimal price impact
- **PT price converges to 1** — guaranteed redemption value

---

## Implied Yield

The AMM expresses prices as **implied yield** — the annualized return if you hold PT to maturity:

<Formula display>{"\\text{lnImpliedRate} = \\ln(\\text{exchangeRate}) \\times \\frac{\\text{SECONDS\\_PER\\_YEAR}}{\\text{timeToExpiry}}"}</Formula>

This implied rate is updated after every trade and used to recalculate the rate anchor, ensuring price continuity.

**Example:** PT at 0.95 with 180 days to expiry = ~10.8% implied APY

---

## Time Decay

As maturity approaches:

1. **PT price rises toward 1** — guaranteed convergence
2. **Price sensitivity decreases** — trades have less impact
3. **YT price falls toward 0** — yield rights worth less

<Callout type="info">
This is automatic. The logit curve naturally pushes PT toward its redemption value as the rate scalar increases near expiry.
</Callout>

---

## Fee Decay

Swap fees decay exponentially toward expiry, matching the time-decay of the underlying assets:

<Formula display>{"\\text{adjustedFeeRate} = e^{\\text{lnFeeRateRoot} \\times \\frac{\\text{timeToExpiry}}{\\text{SECONDS\\_PER\\_YEAR}}}"}</Formula>

This means:
- **1 year out** → Full fee rate applies
- **6 months out** → ~70% of base fee (exponential decay)
- **At expiry** → Minimal fees

This encourages trading near maturity and reduces friction for arbitrageurs maintaining the PT = 1 peg.

---

## Price Impact

Large trades move the implied yield significantly. The logit curve provides natural price impact:
- Small trades near equilibrium have minimal impact
- Large trades that push proportion toward extremes face increasing resistance

**Before trading, check:**
- Spot price vs execution price
- Price impact percentage
- Slippage tolerance setting

<Callout type="warning">
High price impact means you're getting a worse rate. Consider splitting large trades.
</Callout>

## For Liquidity Providers

When you add liquidity:
- Deposit both PT and SY
- Receive LP tokens
- Earn fees from every swap

**LP returns come from:**
- Swap fees (time-adjusted, decays toward expiry)
- Underlying yield (SY keeps earning)
- PT appreciation toward maturity
