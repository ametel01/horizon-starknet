# Trade Yield

Buy or sell exposure to future yield movements using PT and YT.

---

## When to Use This Strategy

**Buy YT when:**
- You expect yields to **stay high or increase**
- You want **leveraged** yield exposure

**Buy PT (sell YT) when:**
- You expect yields to **drop**
- You want **fixed, predictable** returns

---

## Trading PT

### To Buy PT (bet on lower rates)

1. Go to **Trade**
2. Select market
3. Choose **SY → PT** swap direction
4. Enter amount
5. Review price impact and confirm

### To Sell PT (bet on higher rates)

1. Go to **Trade**
2. Select **PT → SY** swap direction
3. Enter amount and confirm

---

## Trading YT

<Callout type="info">
YT trades are executed as PT swaps in reverse. When you buy YT, the protocol buys PT and pairs it with your SY to mint YT.
</Callout>

### To Buy YT

1. Go to **Trade**
2. Select market
3. Choose **Buy YT** tab
4. Enter amount
5. Review and confirm

### To Sell YT

1. Go to **Trade**
2. Select **Sell YT** tab
3. Enter amount and confirm

## Swap with Any Token

<Callout type="info">
This feature requires **Advanced mode**. Enable it in Settings before proceeding.
</Callout>

The token aggregator lets you swap from any supported ERC20 token directly to PT, YT, or SY — and vice versa. No need to manually convert to SY first.

### How It Works

1. Go to **Trade**
2. Toggle **Any Token** mode (appears in Advanced mode)
3. Select your input token from the dropdown
4. Choose your output (PT, YT, or SY)
5. Enter amount — the aggregator finds the best route automatically
6. Review the route preview and confirm

### Route Finding

The aggregator searches across available liquidity sources to find the optimal path. For example, swapping USDC → PT might route through:

```
USDC → underlying asset → SY → PT
```

<Callout type="warning">
Multi-hop routes may have higher price impact. Always review the route preview before confirming.
</Callout>

---

## Tips

- Check implied yield vs underlying yield before trading
- Set reasonable slippage (0.5-1% for most trades)
- Monitor your YT positions — they expire at maturity
